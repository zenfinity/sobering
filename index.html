Sobering Look at Alcohol Use

According to the CDC, 1 in 5 people age 19-40 die of alcohol related causes. $35B is spent annually in the US on care for alcohol use and use disorder, but still 88k people die every year. Globally, alcohol and drug effects is the 4th leading cause of preventable death in OECD countries.

The smallest populace States have the highest average consumption. The highest is New Hampshire, ~17 L/yr. The lowest in the world is Afghanistan, ~0.02 L/yr.

Consumption in Units of Blood

A unit of blood is ~500 mL (one-half a liter, or one pint). The highest alcohol consumers imbibe around triple the volume equivalent of blood in their bodies yearly, ~15L (3gal).
